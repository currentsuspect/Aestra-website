# Aestra security policy

## Reporting a vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability in Aestra (the DAW, the website, or any of our services), please report it privately.

- **Email:** security@aestra.studio
- **GitHub Security Advisories:** https://github.com/currentsuspect/Aestra/security/advisories/new
- **Response time:** within 72 hours

## Scope

In scope:
- aestra.studio and all subdomains
- The Aestra DAW (download, updates, plugin suite)
- The Aestra account system (when available)

Out of scope:
- Third-party plugins hosted by the user
- Third-party websites that link to us
- Social engineering attacks against our team

## Bug bounty

We do not currently run a paid bug bounty. We will, however:
- Credit you in the release notes (with permission)
- Ship you a Founder Gold Card (with permission)
- Be very grateful

## Coordinated disclosure

We follow coordinated disclosure. Please do not publicly disclose a vulnerability until we have shipped a fix or 90 days have passed, whichever comes first.

## PGP key

Available on request. A signed public key will be published at https://aestra.studio/.well-known/pgp-key.asc when available.
